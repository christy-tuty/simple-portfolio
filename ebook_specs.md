## User Recommendations

### 1. The Casual Reader: **Kindle Paperwhite (12th Gen)**
*   **Why:** If you just want to read text-based books and want the best contrast, the new Paperwhite's Carta 1300 screen is the gold standard for B&W reading. It is also the most affordable and offers the longest battery life.
*   **Best for:** Commuters, beach readers (due to waterproofing), and those heavily invested in the Amazon/Goodreads ecosystem.

### 2. The Manga & Graphic Novel Enthusiast: **Boox Go Color 7**
*   **Why:** Manga and comics look great on the Kaleido 3 color screen. More importantly, because it runs **Android**, you can install apps like Shonen Jump, MangaDex, or Tachiyomi directly, which isn't possible on Kindle or Kobo. The expandable storage is also crucial for large image-heavy libraries.
*   **Best for:** Power users who want "one device for all apps" and those who need massive storage.

### 3. The Note-Taker & Annotator: **Kobo Libra Colour**
*   **Why:** It strikes a perfect balance between a traditional e-reader and a digital notebook. With Kobo Stylus 2 support, you can highlight and annotate books in color. It is also waterproof, unlike the Boox, making it more durable for everyday carry.
*   **Best for:** Students, researchers, and readers who like to journal or "markup" their books as they read.

### 4. The Library Power User: **Kobo Libra Colour**
*   **Why:** Kobo has the best native integration with **OverDrive/Libby**, allowing you to borrow library books directly on the device without needing a phone or computer to "send" the book (unlike Kindle in many regions).
*   **Best for:** Readers who primarily get their books from public libraries.
