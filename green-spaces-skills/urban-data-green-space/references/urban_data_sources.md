# Urban Data Sources for Green Space Research

This reference provides a directory of data sources essential for urban green space analysis.

## 1. Global Datasets
- **OpenStreetMap (OSM)**: Excellent for identifying current parks, gardens, and green areas using tags like `leisure=park`, `landuse=grass`.
- **World Bank Open Data**: For macro-level urban development and sustainability indicators.
- **Global Forest Watch**: For canopy cover and deforestation tracking.

## 2. Environmental & Climate Data
- **Copernicus Land Monitoring Service**: High-resolution land cover data.
- **NASA EarthData (MODIS/Landsat)**: For calculating Land Surface Temperature (LST) and Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI).
- **AQICN (Air Quality Index)**: To correlate green spaces with air quality improvements.

## 3. Local/Thai Data Portals
- **Open Data Thailand (data.go.th)**: Search for "Green Space" or "Public Parks" data provided by BMA or local provinces.
- **GIS Portal (GISTDA)**: Satellite imagery and urban heat maps for Thailand.
- **Bangkok Open Data**: Specific datasets on BMA-managed parks and public lands.

## 4. Tools for Data Extraction
- **Overpass Turbo**: For querying specific OSM data.
- **Google Earth Engine**: For large-scale environmental time-series analysis.
