# Green Space Development Mechanisms & Frameworks

This reference outlines the scientific and urban planning mechanisms used to evaluate and develop public green spaces.

## 1. The 3-30-300 Rule
A globally recognized baseline for urban forestry and mental health:
- **3 Trees**: Every citizen should be able to see at least 3 well-established trees from their home.
- **30 Percent**: Every neighborhood should have at least 30% tree canopy cover.
- **300 Meters**: Every citizen should live within 300 meters of a high-quality public green space (approx. 5-minute walk).

## 2. Ecosystem Services Valuation
Measuring the "hidden" benefits of green spaces:
- **Carbon Sequestration**: Calculating tons of CO2 absorbed per hectare.
- **Cooling Effect**: Measuring the reduction in Land Surface Temperature (LST) due to evapotranspiration and shading.
- **Stormwater Management**: Estimating the reduction in runoff and flood risk.

## 3. Equity & Accessibility Metrics
- **Green Space per Capita**: Aiming for the WHO recommendation of 9m² of green space per person.
- **Walkability Index**: Combining street connectivity with green space proximity to measure ease of access.
- **Social Vulnerability Index (SVI)**: Prioritizing green space development in areas with high poverty and low current canopy cover.

## 4. Pocket Park Methodology
Small-scale interventions for dense urban areas:
- **Site Selection**: Focus on underutilized "grey spaces" (alleys, vacant lots, parking zones).
- **Design Mechanism**: Integrating vertical greenery and permeable paving to maximize impact in small areas.
